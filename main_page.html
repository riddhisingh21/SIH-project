Bridging the Communication Gap

Using ISL to Text and Speech Technology for a More Inclusive World

The Problem People Face

Every day, individuals who are deaf or hard of hearing face communication challenges in both personal and professional settings. Indian Sign Language (ISL) users often struggle to engage in simple conversations because most people are unfamiliar with ISL. Whether it's in education, healthcare, or daily interactions, the inability to communicate efficiently can lead to feelings of isolation and misunderstanding.

Traditional solutions, such as interpreters or written communication, are not always available or effective, especially in fast-paced environments. This communication gap limits opportunities and creates barriers to participation in society.

How We Solve It

Our solution transforms ISL into text and speech, making communication more accessible for those who are hearing impaired. Through this tool, users can translate their hand gestures into real-time text or audio output. This ensures that individuals who don't know ISL can still understand and communicate with ISL users, breaking down communication barriers and fostering inclusivity in all walks of life.

Whether in classrooms, workplaces, or everyday scenarios, this tool offers a seamless way to connect and engage with the world around them. We believe in empowering everyone with the ability to communicate effectively, regardless of their hearing ability.
